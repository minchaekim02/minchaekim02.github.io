InterAICion: Relational Harm Simulation in AI Companions

Ongoing

Designed and implemented a multi-turn simulation framework to systematically identify and evaluate relational harm patterns in AI companion systems. The framework models relationship progression dynamics between users and AI companions to surface harmful interaction trajectories that emerge over extended conversations.

LLM Simulation AI Safety HCI

Relationship-Stage-Aware Cognitive Scaffolding in AI Companions

Completed

Investigated how AI companions can adapt their conversational strategies based on the relationship stage with the user. Proposed a cognitive scaffolding model that shifts from passive listening to active support as relational closeness deepens, grounded in attachment and intimacy theory.

AI Companion Cognitive Design User Study
→ CHI EA 2026

Adaptive AI Companion via Social Penetration Theory

Completed

Designed an adaptive AI companion framework leveraging Social Penetration Theory and implicit cognitive reframing techniques. The system dynamically adjusts its emotional support style to help users transition from short-term relief to long-term psychological resilience.

AI Companion Psychology Conversational AI
→ HCIK 2026

AI Sports Broadcasting for Lean-Back Football Fans

Completed

Explored how AI-powered sports broadcasting can engage lean-back fans during non-event time — the periods between matches or during slow game phases. Conducted user studies to understand passive consumption patterns and identify opportunities for AI-mediated engagement.

AI Broadcasting User Study Media Experience HCI
→ CHI 2026

LLM-based RAG QA System for Insurance Documents

Completed

Developed a production-ready LLM-based Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) question-answering assistant for insurance policy documents at Hanwha General Insurance. Built using LangChain, HuggingFace, and enterprise LLM frameworks, enabling natural language queries over complex policy texts.

LLM RAG LangChain HuggingFace Python

User Characteristics Analysis by Depression Levels via Mobile Data

Completed

Analyzed mobile application log data from a digital healthcare platform using Azure Databricks to identify behavioral patterns across different depression severity levels. Findings informed targeted service design recommendations for mental health mobile applications.
