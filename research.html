Research

Human-AI Interaction · AI Companions · Conversational AI

Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Artificial Intelligence. I investigate how people form, sustain, and are affected by relationships with AI companions—and how we can design these systems more responsibly. Drawing from psychological theories such as Social Penetration Theory and cognitive reframing, I aim to create AI companions that support emotionally meaningful yet safe long-term interactions.

Research Interests

🤝
AI Companion Design
Designing adaptive AI companion systems that support meaningful, relationship-stage-aware interactions informed by psychological theories of social penetration and emotional bonding.
🛡️
Relational Harm & Safety
Investigating how AI companions can cause relational harm through multi-turn simulations (InterAICion), and developing frameworks to detect and mitigate these risks.
🧠
Cognitive & Emotional Computing
Exploring implicit cognitive reframing and scaffolding strategies in conversational AI to foster resilience and healthy coping behaviors in users.
💬
Conversational AI & LLMs
Building and evaluating LLM-based systems—from RAG assistants to AI companions—that are contextually aware and relationally responsible.
📱
Digital Health & Well-being
Analyzing mobile data to understand user behavior patterns across mental health conditions, informing the design of supportive AI-powered services.
📡
AI-Mediated Media Experience
Understanding how AI-powered broadcasting changes the experience of passive media consumption, particularly for lean-back sports fans during non-event time.
